RECENT POEMS

After “Processional” by Joanna Klink

If you love me,
let marigolds grow in your hair
let my fingers fly and my toes explore
through dirt and sand and water.
Let me miss you out loud
and sink to the bottom of a shallow creek,
hold my breath for three minutes
and find my way back to the surface
having befriended the tadpoles.
Let me dance up red and purple
rocks and line them down your spine,
let me write epics on your knees.
If you love me, eat mangoes
walk barefoot
stretch and sing with yourself
and see orange in the morning.
If you love me,
walk two steps ahead
give me a room made of windows
and leave me with the light.



Time Travel

Once, a dream,
Evaporating back and forth
(from Brooklyn to
Queens) I would appear
in your bed
and oyster around you
sheets soft as waves
clutching us to sleep.

Then in a past
we talked time
holding the moment in space
(it's smaller than a pearl
between our bodies)
hiding the minutes in our shoulders
hips kissing goodnight too
early for my taste.

Now we dance
(present, together)
arms around my room
a splash of moonlight
on our faces,
letting night be hours or days.

However long we have is just
almost enough.
